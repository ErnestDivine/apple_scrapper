    Description

    Put convenient healthcare in your hands—and in your phone—with the free Everlywell App.


    At Everlywell, we believe taking action on your health and wellness should be affordable, easy, and keep you feeling good.


    The free Everlywell App provides a seamless, secure, and simple at-home health care experience— wherever you are.


    TELEHEALTH WHEN (AND WHERE) YOU NEED IT

    Sick and tired of being sick and tired? We got you. Get care for your concerns from the comfort of home with our new telehealth offering (cash pay and insurance payment options available). Join appointments, view care plans, and more right from the Everlywell App.


    Support includes:

    • View upcoming and completed appointments

    • Schedule and join appointments

    • View care plans

    • Get push notifications about appointments and care plans


    MORE COLLECTION SUPPORT FOR YOUR EVERLYWELL TESTS

    We know that collecting your own sample may be a new (and exciting!) experience, but with our network of partner labs processing nearly 900,000 blood collection cards in a given year, the Everlywell App can provide you with our collection expertise along the way.


    Support includes:

    • Collection tips with the best times of day to collect your sample

    • Reminders unique to your test, so you never miss a step or collection window

    • The use of Everlywell’s Spotwell Technology: For certain tests, you can use the scanner on your phone’s camera to provide you with instant feedback on your blood spot card to guarantee accurate collection

    • Access to our Customer Care team to have your questions answered


    With convenient telehealth, a suite of vitamins and supplements, and over 30+ at-home lab tests with physician-reviewed results, your healthier tomorrow can start today. You’ve got this!

    Worthless

    This app functions well, but too bad it is for a worthless product. It does not provide numbers, just a positive (or negative) response. The “support” they offer is a call center rep telling you that the results are positive and go to the doctor. I didn’t ask for a definitive diagnosis, but selling this as offering support is deceptive marketing, if not outright lying. You can talk to a “provider” but it is just a mid-level who cannot diagnose. I can read “positive” and “talk to a doctor” without a call center rep or paying a midlevel to read it to me. What is the point of a test not providing numbers and ranges?! Are they just randomly throwing out positives and negatives in results?! Further, my doctor (actual DO who uses a reputable lab) repeated the testing and my results were NOT positive, not even close to positive. In fact, not only was I NOT positive on the one thing they said I was, I WAS positive on the thing they said I was not! Total joke and waste of money. Very deceptive marketing. Other reputable labs will do many of these same tests for cash pay and actually provide numbers and resources to understand numbers. What a joke.
